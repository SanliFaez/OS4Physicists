# Open Science for Physicists
*Syllabus*

+ Last updated Date: 6/JUN/2024
+ Lecturers: Sanli Faez @SanliFaez, Erik van Sebille @erikvansebille, Tanja Hinderer
+ Tutorials: Sjoerd Terpstra, Marc Schneiter, Hossein Nemati

This course is presented for the second time in the academic year 2024/25 as a compulsary course for all three track in the Master of Physics programs at Utrecht University. 
Some parts of the course might be adjusted or reshuffled by the lecturers, *during* the execution.  


## Week 1: Why open science? How did we get here?  

+ The social origins of unreliable scientific output
+ Introduction to git and gitlab 

*keywords:* Five schools of Open Science, Open Science achievements, Credibility cycle, The reproducibility crisis, Fraud and Questionable Research Practices, Global perspectives on open science  

*keywords not covered:* Amateurism, Access and Emancipation, Community-Discipline-Employer, Transdsiciplinary and interdisciplinary science

## Week 2: Setting up a collaborative environment

+ Git for collaboration

*keywords:* Version control, Licensing and recieving credit, Readme files and templates, Best practices for community management, Merging repositories 

*keywords not covered:* Active and passive versioning, Project management and Getting Things Done (GTD), Unit testing (defensive programming), Scripting, Compatibility, Dockers-Virtual Environments-Pinning, Best practices in data visualization,

## Week 3: Big Science 

+ Team structures and open science adaptation in big collaboration

*keywords:* Big-science collaborations, Credit and recognition, Governance structures, Independence and thriving, 

*keywords not covered:* Citizen science, Policy and politics of science, Relativism of big, Climate impacts and transformation of the research ecosystem

## Week 4: Open innovation and open source hardware

+ The open science route to knowledge transfer

*keywords:* Community projects, Technology transfer cycle, Best practices for documentation, templates and bug reports, Microcontrollers, Remote labs, Maintenance, Prototyping

*keywords not covered:* Data structures and data plans, GDPR and privacy rules, Persisten identifiers, Machine-readable docs

## Week 5: Knowledge discovery

+ AI assisted reviews

*keywords:* Scientific design environments, Open protocols, Automation and bots,  

*keywords not covered:* Materials-resources-constraints, Defense and national security, 

## Week 6: Public engagement, activism and trust in scientists
*keywords:* How storytelling works, Science communication and journalism, Activism and trust in Science, Bounds of expertise, Academic social contract, Metaphores, 

*keywords not covered:* Communicating uncertainty, Narratives and framing, Difficult to reach audience, Process and outcome

## Week 7: Reproducibility

+ Designing for reproducibility

*keywords:*  Open-Licensing strategies, Data plans, Documentation, Project maintenance, Data stewardship, 

## Week 8: Reflections, beyond the PhD

*keywords not covered:* Careers in science and beyond, Applying for scientif positions, Grant writing, the PhD advantage, Mental health in academia, Preprinting and peer-review,  CV and applications, Altmetrics and visibiity, Recognition and rewards, Literature and referencing, Science in transition
