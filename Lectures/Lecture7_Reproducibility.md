## Assignments

**Assignment 1 objective**: 
Analyzing [typical cases](https://tinyurl.com/OS4PCOPY) for legal reuse of materials and data. Assess what is possible or not in a real life case, reckoning with copyright

**Assignment description**

1. Are you disclosing (parts of) works to the public or reproducing (parts of) works?
2. Is what is being reused a work in the sense of the Dutch copyright law?
3. Is it (still) copyrighted?
4. Who is the holder of the copyright?
5. Is there a limitation on copyright applicable? (onderwijsexceptie, citaatrecht, kopie eigen gebruik) (=educational exception, citation exception, copy for personal use)
6. What are the terms of use? Is there any reuse license?
7. Fallback option(s): how can you remain compliant by changing your plans (ask permission, not sharing, just linking, alternative source)
8. What to do to adhere to the Dutch code of conduct for research integrity?


**Assignment submission**: 
Students submit a comment to Issue #29 after answering the above questions. Please, use these headlines as a template:

1. Disclosure/reproduction?
2. Work?
3. Copyrighted?
4. Holder of copyright?
5. Limitations?
6. Terms of use / Reuse license?
7. Fallback options?
8. Academic integrity


**Assignment 2 objective**: 

Goal: describe in 150-300 words a hypothetical sharing/reusing case you might find your future selves in, as researchers or entrepreneurs working on a project

**State at least:**
+ who is working on the hypothetical project
+ what is to goal of the project
+ an example of what is being shared and/or reused in the project
+ who has copyright on the reused work, how it was licensed, how it was reused, 
+ who has copyright over the final product and how and with which license that is being shared
+ any specific circumstances you think you need to reckon with

Submit you case as a comment under issue #30.
