# CODE OF CONDUCT 2023

This code of conduct will been decided by cohort participants in Open Science for Physicists (NS-PH500M) and is enacted from 18112023.
The procedure is based on [this exercise by Aurelia Moser](http://aureliamoser.com/aaas-guides/conduct/index.html)  

Everyone taking part in the course and discussions (mentors, helpers, coordinators, and learners) is required to conform to the following Code of Conduct. Coordinators will oversee adherence to this code throughout the course.
 
#### Characteristics we value:
- Punctuality
- Honesty
- Respect
- Kindness
- Understanding
- Open-mindedness
- Curiosity and willingness to learn
- Optimism
- Transparency
- Inclusiveness
- Professionalism
- Imagination
- Patience
- Integrity
- Critical thinking
- Constructive self-criticism


#### Behaviors we encourage:
- Asking questions
- Being flexible
- Taking initiative
- Accountability
- Good vibes
- Sticking to agreements and deadlines
- Sharing concerns
- Paying attention
- Not avoiding confrontations, but actively seeking to compromise or create mutual understanding
- Taking it easy when opinions differ
- Respecting personal boundaries
- Letting people finish talking
- Showing appreciation

#### Behaviors we discourage? 
- Arrogance
- Selfishness and individualism
- Not being open to other people's point of view
- Not being open to work together
- Being unfriendly, impolite or standoffish
- Discrimination
- Treating differently based on gender identity
- Personal attacks or bullying
- Laughing at others' suggestions
- Harassment
- Verbal and physical assault
- Blaming people
- Being judgemental
- Being mean or showing hostility
- Being disrespectful
- Crossing other people's boundaries
- Not taking each other seriously
- Escalating an argument
- Dishonesty
- Lying about your progress
- Plagiarism
- Interrupting and talking over people
- Interdisciplinary negligence as a result of disciplinary elitism
- Distracting other's from activaly participating
- Micromanaging

We discourage people from not actively participating in this course, for example through:
- Not reacting to people's reasonable inquiries (going radio silent)
- Avoiding doing any work
- Freeloading (i.e. not putting in the work or taking credit for other people's work)
- Being late to the class

#### We make each others feel safe and supported by

- Accepting diversity
- Encouraging equal participation
- Giving people space for their mental well-being, both personally and academically
- Adjusting your communication to the person you are addressing (empathetic communication)
- Creating a collaborative environment
- Creating an environment where humour and bonding activities are possible
- Open communications
- Helping each other out and be open to being helped
- Supporting individual choices
- Leaving room for failure
- Being relaxed and polite when holding a discussion
- Respecting the person even if you do not agree with their opinion
- Being open for discussion despite the background of the question's origin
- Letting everybody speak
- Taking people seriously and not being condescending
- Letting people finish speaking without interrupting
- Respecting everybody's boundaries
- Access to confidential counselling (either within our outside of this course, see also below)

We also value each other's contributions by:
- Making use of other's ideas
- Giving credit for everyone's ideas and contributions

#### How to report an issue, should someone violate the code? 

1. Contact Sanli Faez (@Sanli) or Erik van Sebille by private message, or in person. All communication will be treated as confidential. 
2. If for any reason you don’t want to do 1, you can contact Peter van der Straten who has agreed to function as external contact person for this purpose.

## Community Participation Guidelines
Apart from the code of conduct, all participants in this course must follow these general guidelines.

### Participation Guidelines

When participating in OS4P, respect the Utrecht University guideline for [academic and scientific integrity](https://www.uu.nl/en/files/netherlands-code-of-conduct-for-research-integrity2018ukpdf) and [code of conduct](https://www.uu.nl/en/files/codeofconductuuenpdf). These guidelines cover our behaviour as participants, mentors, experts, staff, volunteers, and anyone else involved in making this course possible.

### How to treat each other

To create a collaborative and inviting learning environment, we also emphasise certain values in how we treat each other:

+ Be respectful and value each other’s ideas, styles and viewpoints.  
+ Be direct but professional; we cannot withhold hard truths.  
+ Be inclusive and help new perspectives be heard.  
+ Appreciate and accommodate our many cultural practices, attitudes and beliefs.  
+ Be open to learning from others.  
+ Lead by example and match your actions with your words.  

The following will not be tolerated during the activities related to this course: 

+ violence and threats of violence
+ personal attacks; derogatory language
+ unwelcome sexual attention or physical contact
+ disruptive behaviour
+ influencing unacceptable behaviour.

### Inclusion and Diversity

We welcome contributions from everyone as long as they interact constructively with our community, including, but not limited to people of varied age, culture, ethnicity, gender, gender-identity, language, race, sexual orientation, geographical location and religious views.

### Raising Issues
If you believe you‘re experiencing practices which don‘t meet the above policies, or if you feel you are being harassed in any way, please immediately contact the course coordinator Sanli Faez, or the university Academic Integrity Counsellor. [Prof. Liesbeth Woertman](https://www.uu.nl/staff/emwoertman&t=0).

The course coordinators reserve the right to refuse admission to anyone violating these policies, and/or take further action including reporting to the responsible figure for academic integrity at the department.
